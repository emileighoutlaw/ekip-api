Privacy policy

Every Kid in a Park is a new initiative from the Department of Interior that aims to give every fourth grader in the country an opportunity to gain free entry into the national parks, historic structures, cultural artifacts, ancient forests, snow-capped mountains, and clear blue lakes across the country.

Every Kid in a Park privacy policy

Every Kid in a Park does not ask for or collect personal, individually-identifiable information from kids. We request users provide their ZIP code for analytics purposes and to generate a ticket for entrance into National Parks and Public Lands. We also collect other analytics so we can learn about our visitors, but we can't tell personal information from them. We do not create individual profiles from the information provided or distribute the information to other organizations.

Information collected and stored automatically

We will collect no personal information about you when you visit our website. We collect and store only the following information about you:

  • ZIP code
  • IP address
  • Aggregate web browser statistics (for example, your web browser version, your operating system type, and other general details)

We use the information we collect to measure the number and type of visitors to the different sections of our site so that we can make our site more useful.

Security notice

For site security purposes and to ensure that this service remains available to all users, Every Kid in a Park follows industry-standard methods to monitor network traffic to identify unauthorized attempts to upload or change information, or otherwise cause damage. Unauthorized attempts to upload information or change information on DOI servers are strictly prohibited and may be punishable by law, including the Computer Fraud and Abuse Act of 1986 and the National Information Infrastructure Protection Act. In the specific context of this security monitoring, there is no expectation of privacy. However, only in the case of actual law enforcement investigations, will we attempt to identify individual users or their usage habits.
